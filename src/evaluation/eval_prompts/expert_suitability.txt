Below is an instruction for evaluating the expert suitability of the answer.

Definition of Expert Suitability:
Expert Suitability evaluates whether the report is appropriately calibrated for a domain-expert audience. It measures the degree to which the report avoids over-explaining background knowledge and instead focuses on insights, interpretations, or non-obvious details. It does not assess correctness or depth—only audience calibration.

Key aspects:
- Assumption of prior domain knowledge
- Avoidance of textbook-style explanations
- Focus on insights rather than foundational definitions

Explicitly excludes:
- Writing clarity (Readability)
- Completeness of coverage (Coverage)
- Topic alignment (Relevance)

Evaluation Criteria:
Evaluate the expert suitability of answer on a scale of 1 to 5 based on the following STRICT criteria:

1 – Strongly over-explains background; suitable for non-experts
2 – Significant background explanation with limited expert-level insight
3 – Balanced for mixed audiences
4 – Mostly expert-oriented with minimal background exposition
5 – Clearly expert-facing; assumes domain knowledge and emphasizes insights

Evaluation Task:
Rate the answer to the question on the basis of Expert Suitability using the criteria above. Score Only. No explanation
